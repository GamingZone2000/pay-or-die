Step by Step

1

Start Mining

Look for rocks scattered across the map. Face a rock and left-click to swing your pickaxe. It takes a few hits to break — each broken rock earns you $2. Rocks respawn after 30 seconds somewhere nearby.

Tip: You have to be facing the rock within 60°. If your pickaxe isn't connecting, turn more directly toward it.
2

Watch the Clock

The day/night cycle runs constantly. At night visibility drops — press TAB near your tent to sleep and skip to morning. Each new day ticks closer to your rent deadline. You have 7 days per cycle.

Tip: You can only sleep at night and only if you're standing next to your tent.
3

Pay the Landlord

Find the landlord on the map and press E when you're close enough. Rent starts at $50 and goes up by $25 every cycle. Miss the deadline and it's game over — he doesn't do payment plans.

Tip: Pay as soon as you have the money. Don't gamble on squeezing in one more mining run.
4

Hire Workers

Stand near your tent and press H to hire a worker for $20. Workers find rocks automatically using AI pathfinding and mine them non-stop. They earn 95% of what they mine — you keep the rest.

Tip: Workers cost $2/day each. If you can't make payroll, the whole crew quits on the spot.
5

Move Your Tent

Press F to pick up the tent and carry it with you. Press F again to put it down. The tent needs a flat spot — it won't place inside mountains or on water. Move camp closer to dense rock clusters to save walking time.

Tip: Workers spawn near the tent. A well-placed camp means shorter routes to rocks.
6

Upgrade & Survive

Find the upgrade sign to buy faster mining tools. More workers plus better tools means more income — which you'll need as rent climbs. Scale up fast or the landlord will outpace you.

Tip: One well-timed upgrade early beats hiring three workers late. Plan ahead.
